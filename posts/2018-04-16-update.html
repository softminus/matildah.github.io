Since last time

I did get out of the truly awful slumlord situation, but I don’t have a stable place to live. During the last part of 2017 I was out of the country to visit someone who unfortunately is now an ex. I then temporarily stayed with an acquaintance in Seattle, and in January 2018 I moved to to Los Angeles and stayed with a friend, who could only accommodate me for three months. During that period of relative stability I was able to start improving my mental health, developing a routine and good working habits as well as some new technical skills: I designed my first PCB, got an iCE40 devboard and learned Verilog 1, and brought my understanding of DSP math from “shaky intuitions” to “demodulating DPSK signals from aircraft”2 while beginning the design and construction of an ADS-B/Mode-S aircraft transponder.

In April I had to move out and I am struggling to find new housing.  My mental health situation has deteriorated markedly as a consequence, and my ability to stay focused (already tenuous due to ADHD) on technical work and other important tasks, including the housing search itself,  has been decimated due to this instability.

I’d like to stay vaguely in the LA area because I’m here already so it’s easier logistically, and I have a couple of IRL friends here that can help me stay on track. I also have access to tools and test equipment to continue the electronics projects I’ve been working on.

I don’t have exorbitant needs for a place to live: as long as the rent is reasonable, I have a room of my own3 and access to a kitchen, and the other roommates are reasonable and polite, I should be fine. However, in the past I’ve tried relaxing those requirements and it’s led to utter misery and dysfunction (see prior post) so they are strict requirements.

I’m decently talented in some technical fields and I know I have potential, but at the moment my mental health isn’t yet stable enough for me to be employable. I’ve only just gotten Medicaid and haven’t yet found a good therapist knowledgeable about ADHD/ASD issues that accepts it.

Without proof of income, and with social anxiety making for awkward interactions with potential landlords, along with demoralizing anti-trans prejudice I have had no success finding housing so far by conventional means.  I’ve tried calling and emailing strangers off craigslist and I’ve visited a handful and they never get back to me or tell me that they chose to rent to someone else. The waiting lists for subsidized housing are many years long if not closed altogether.

I really need help finding a place to live. The situation for me is very bad and getting worse. I have about $3k in savings which should be enough for a deposit, but I’m burning through it just keeping a cheap airbnb roof over my head. I do have a small stipend from a family member and could afford up to about $600/month in rent.
